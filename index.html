A day out to Haarlem center and surroundings

Will it be a day full of art and culture? Do you want to know more about the history of the city? Or are you coming to Haarlem to shop extensively? Getting bored is simply not an option here, because in this historic city full of monuments, restaurants, shops, water, squares and parks you can literally go in all directions. Wander through the hidden courtyards of Haarlem. Discover what the Frans Hals masterpieces look like up close. Walk past the boutiques in the Golden Streets. Rent a sloop and explore Haarlem from the canals. Step inside the impressive Grote of St. Bavo church and admire the organ that Mozart once played. Or find a place on one of the cozy terraces for a cup of coffee, lunch, drink or dinner.

Visit the surroundings of Haarlem? Take a walk through the Haarlemmerhout, go to the picturesque village of Spaarndam or get on a bike and drive through National Park Zuid-Kennemerland to the beaches of Bloemendaal and Zandvoort.
Centrum

Downtown
In the heart of the city center lies the Grote Markt in the city center, which houses a number of special historic buildings, including the town hall , the Vleeshal and the Grote or St. Bavokerk . The downtown skyline is dominated by this huge church with its almost 80 meter high tower. Many famous musicians, including Mozart, may have played in the world-famous MÃ¼ller organ in the church. There is also the school building of the Stedelijk Gymnasium Haarlem , one of the earliest schools. In addition, the third version of the first Dutch aircraft, the Spin 3 by Anthony Fokker, flew, on a flight above the center of Haarlem and around the St. Bavo church on the occasion of Queen's Day in 1911. There is a market twice a week on the Grote Markt and various events take place throughout the year, including the annual fair. Important shopping streets run from Grote Markt in three directions, namely Grote Houtstraat , Barteljorisstraat and Zijlstraat . There are a number of small, characteristic shopping streets in the historic center, such as Warmoesstraat, Schagchelstraat and Kleine Houtstraat.

Burgwal
The Spaarne River divides the city center into two parts. West of the river is the largest part of the city center. To the east the part that is also called the Spaarnwouderbuurt . In this part, among other things, the only remaining city gate of Haarlem is the Spaarnwouder or Amsterdamse Poort. The mill De Adriaan and Koepelgevangenis are also located here . Both banks of the Spaarne are connected by two monumental swing bridges, the Catharijnebrug and the Melkbrug and two drawbridges including the Gravestenenbrug, and a modern drawbridge. This bridge, popularly called the 'Verfroller', is officially called the Long Bridge . In the past there was also the Sint Nicolaas or Coral bridge between the Houtmarkt and the Koudehorn, but this has disappeared. There were plans to bring this bridge back.
Many of the buildings in the city center date from the 16th, 17th, 18th and 19th centuries. However, there is also a lot of modern architecture, such as the Toneelschuur and the new building around the Appelaar .

Hwa-Rang Dragon
Taekwondo is originally a Korean martial art, where both hand and foot techniques are used. The literal translation of the word Taekwondo is: "The way of the foot and the fist". The word Taekwondo is made up of 3 parts: Tae (foot). The foot represents all leg techniques such as kicking and jumping. Kwon (fist). The hand stands for all hand and arm techniques such as punching, blocking, grasping, clamping and throwing. Do (the way). The road is the path that you follow while practicing Taekwondo and mainly represents the mental part.

TAEKWONDO FOR CHILDREN

We offer children lessons at their own level! We divide the children into age-specific groups. That happens in class, but also with separate hours. In this way a child gets the attention it needs and to evolve further. With us, taekwondo is more than kicking. For thousands of years, martial art has been used for so much more than just self-defense. It helps children with discipline and focus. They are more concentrated at school, gain self-confidence and have respect for their parents and others. Every lesson the children train their fitness in a fun way. Taekwondo keeps them healthy and strong, develops their balance, coordination and strength. Taekwondo is offered by us for every level and every age (from 7 years).

TAEKWONDO FOR ADULTS

The adults can also come to us for an extensive range of training options. From 15 years you can participate in the Taekwondo lessons for adults. In addition to traditional taekwondo, these lessons also include influences from other styles. Continuous work is being done to push boundaries through self-defense, sparring and traditional training. In this way you are challenged to continue to develop. We do all of this in an infectious and positive environment, so that you leave the school full of energy and look forward to the next training.

BASIC TECHNIQUES

The basis of the Taekwondo is formed by a good position and the ability to properly implement all kinds of separate arm and leg techniques. Both beginners and advanced students spend a lot of time and attention learning these basic techniques.

POOMSE, STYLE FORMS

The style forms portray a sham fight. A fixed sequence of attack and defense techniques is then carried out against an imaginary opponent. Mastering style forms shows which skills a Taekwondoin is capable of. Characteristics such as strength, balance, dynamics and appearance must be sufficiently developed. The poomses are therefore an important part of the taekwondo and are central during the tire exam.

KYORUGI, SPARRING

Kyorugi is a part for which many championships are organized. Within Hwa-Rang Dragon, however, we do not only train on Olympic (WTF) firs. The longer you train, the more you practice on spruce with boxing gloves and on ground combat (TKMudo). During sparring, sufficient protection is worn, such as chest, head, crotch, arm and shin instep protectors, a bit and boxing gloves. This protection makes it possible to spar with each other in a safe and responsible manner.

HOSINSUL, SELF DEFENSE

In the hosinsul section, various defenses are practiced against all kinds of attacks with weapons and attacks without weapons, such as seizures or clamps. It is important to know how to fall. That is why we explicitly pay attention to it
